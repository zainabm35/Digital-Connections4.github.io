Day 1 of the Persian Groupchat

Come explore a Persian Groupchat with me filled with strangers from all over the World November 07,2024

The first day! Hey guys! I need your suggestions. I joined a Persian groupchat with people from all over the world. It is somewhat welcoming but these people never stop texting. There is about 150 people in the groupchat and it seems to always be active.Seemed a little risky at first to join a large groupchat like this but I do think it might be a good way to keep myself connected to my persian roots by interacting with other people of the same culture. So far, it definitely feels overwhelming to see so many notifications from instagram at once.

I have't made much of an attempt to join in on the conversations but I'm wondering what are our thoughts on Groupchats like these? Is it possible to find friends and should people even speak in them for their own safety?

1 Week of the Persian Groupchat

Getting to know Eachother Nov 18,2024

-Week 1 Making friends with strangers

A week has gone by now and somehow the group has still managed to be active. The girl who made the groupchat constantly wants to call which has also made me wonder how people are so open to interacting with strangers like that. Nonetheless, I decided to stay away from the calls. I did see that although there was over 200 people at one point, only 20 actually spoke in the groupchat. I eventually felt comfortable and started opening up. I even met someone from New York in the groupchat and we happened to go to the same Persian school as kids which caught us both off gaurd.It amazed how everyone was in different corners of the world yet we still had so much in common surprisingly.

Weeks later

Where are we with the Groupchat now?

New Groupchat? Creating a Groupchat of my own.

Though it was fun being in that groupchat, I had decided my departure time has come. I had never realized how much drama could come from a groupchat consisting of people miles apart from each other. I have made one good friend, Saba, from the groupchat and we collectively decided to create our own small groupchat. We started off with some people from the old groupchat but eventually decided that we wanted to start fresh so we had some of our friends add people they knew as well. I think that we have been successful so far at keeping the groupchat. Let me know your thoughts on creating groupchats below. We named the Groupchat after everyone's least favorite Persian stew, Khoresht karafs which is composed of celery and beef. So far, there has been drama here and there but it seems like everyone enjoys speaking in it and they have all developed a good friendship with each other despite half the groupchat being in America and the other half in Europe and Australia. These aspects are what make the groupchat more interesting as we get to compare how our lives maybe different or similar. Since the timezones are different, the groupchat is never quiet, there is usually someone speaking.
